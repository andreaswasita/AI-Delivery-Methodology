## 4. Resistance Management

### 4.1 Anticipated Resistance

| Source | Reason for Resistance | Level | Mitigation Strategy | Owner | Status |
|--------|----------------------|-------|---------------------|-------|--------|
| End Users | Fear of job loss | High | Communicate augmentation message, show career growth opportunities | CM | |
| Middle Mgmt | Loss of control | Medium | Involve in design, show how it enhances their leadership | BA | |
| IT Ops | Support burden concerns | Medium | Comprehensive training, good documentation, shared responsibility model | PM | |
| Skeptics | Doubt AI will work | Medium | Early demos, quick wins, transparent about limitations | AI Lead | |

---

### 4.2 Resistance Response Plan

**Level 1: Information/Communication**
- Resistance due to lack of information
- Response: Proactive communication, FAQs, town halls

**Level 2: Involvement/Participation**
- Resistance due to not being consulted
- Response: Include in design, UAT, champions program

**Level 3: Negotiation/Agreement**
- Resistance due to concerns about change
- Response: Address concerns, negotiate adjustments, pilot approach

**Level 4: Manipulation/Co-optation**
- Strong resistance from key influencers
- Response: Make them part of solution, give ownership

**Level 5: Explicit/Implicit Coercion**
- Last resort for critical blockers
- Response: Executive intervention, mandate
