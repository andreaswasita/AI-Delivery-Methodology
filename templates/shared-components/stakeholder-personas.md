## PERSONA 1: Executive Sponsor

### Profile Summary

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Typical Roles** | C-Level Executive, VP, SVP, Business Unit President |
| **Department** | Executive Office, Business Unit Leadership |
| **Reports To** | CEO, Board of Directors |

---

### Analysis Dimensions

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|

### Needs & Expectations

#### Primary Needs:
- **Strategic Business Value**: Clear alignment to organizational strategic objectives
- **Financial ROI**: Compelling return on investment (typically 200-400% over 3 years)
- **Risk Mitigation**: Confidence that risks are identified and managed
- **Minimal Disruption**: Project won't create organizational chaos
- **Executive-Level Updates**: High-level dashboards, not detailed status reports
- **Quick Escalation Resolution**: Fast answers when they have questions
- **Organizational Readiness**: Confidence the organization can execute and sustain

#### Success Metrics They Care About:
- Strategic objectives achieved (e.g., "Enable digital transformation")
- Financial ROI (NPV, IRR, payback period)
- Overall risk status (green/yellow/red)
- Milestone achievement (on-time delivery)
- Organizational adoption metrics

### Concerns & Objections

| Concern | Typical Questions | Severity |
|---------|------------------|----------|

| **Organizational Disruption** | "Will this hurt productivity during rollout?" | Medium |
| **Failure Risk** | "What if this doesn't work?" | High |
| **Reputation Risk** | "How will this reflect on me/us?" | Medium |
| **Competing Priorities** | "Why this vs. other strategic initiatives?" | High |

### Engagement Strategy

#### Communication Approach:
- **Frequency**: Monthly steering committee + ad-hoc for critical decisions
- **Duration**: 30-60 minutes per touchpoint
- **Communication Method**:

  - Executive dashboard (1-page, visual, traffic lights)
  - Escalation emails (brief, action-oriented)
  
#### Key Messages to Emphasize:
- **Business Value**: "This drives 30% productivity increase in customer service"
- **Strategic Alignment**: "Directly supports our digital-first strategy"
- **Risk Mitigation**: "We have comprehensive risk management and mitigation plans"
- **Confidence**: "Team is experienced, plan is solid, risks are managed"

- âœ… Benefit realization reviews (post-launch)

#### Do's:
- âœ… Prepare thoroughly for every interaction
- âœ… Be concise and get to the point quickly
- âœ… Present solutions, not just problems
- âœ… Bring data and evidence
- âœ… Respect their time (start/end on time)

#### Don'ts:

---

### Risk if Not Engaged

**Critical Risks:**
- Project cancellation or budget cuts mid-project
- Lack of organizational support and resource commitment
- Inability to remove organizational blockers
- Project deprioritized in favor of competing initiatives
- Perception of project failure even if technically successful

**Mitigation:**
- Secure strong, written commitment before project starts
- Monthly executive steering committee meetings (non-negotiable)
- Weekly executive dashboard/status updates
- Demonstrate quick wins and tangible value early (within 90 days)
- Keep sponsor informed of all major risks proactively
- Build steering committee with multiple executives (backup support)

- Develop detailed runbooks and troubleshooting guides
- Set up robust monitoring and alerting (Azure Monitor, Application Insights)
- Conduct incident simulation exercises before go-live
- Provide hypercare support for first 60 days (24/7 coverage)

## Common Resistance Patterns & Responses

### Resistance Pattern 1: "AI will replace jobs"
- **Who**: End Users, Middle Management
- **Response**: 
  - "This AI augments your expertise, doesn't replace you"
  - "You'll focus on higher-value work that requires human judgment"
  - "Learn valuable AI skills that enhance your career"
  - **Evidence**: Show industry data on augmentation vs. replacement

### Resistance Pattern 2: "This will never work"
- **Who**: Skeptical Business Owners, Technical Staff
- **Response**:
  - Show similar successful case studies from industry
  - Demonstrate working prototypes early
  - Start with achievable goals, build credibility
  - Be transparent about limitations
  - **Evidence**: Pilot results, industry benchmarks

### Resistance Pattern 3: "Not in my budget"
- **Who**: CFO, Business Owner
- **Response**:
  - Present conservative, well-researched business case
  - Show ROI trajectory with realistic assumptions
  - Propose phased approach to spread costs
  - Demonstrate opportunity cost of inaction
  - **Evidence**: ROI calculator, competitor analysis

### Resistance Pattern 4: "We're not ready"
- **Who**: CIO/CTO, IT Operations
- **Response**:
  - Acknowledge concerns and validate them
  - Build comprehensive readiness plan
  - Provide adequate training and documentation
  - Start with pilot to build capability
  - **Evidence**: Readiness assessment, training plan

### Resistance Pattern 5: "Too risky"
- **Who**: Executive Sponsor, Compliance/Legal
- **Response**:
  - Comprehensive risk assessment with mitigations
  - Start with low-risk use case to build confidence
  - Implement strong governance and oversight
  - Show how risks are managed proactively
  - **Evidence**: Risk register, mitigation plans, governance model

## Customization Guide

### For Your Project:
1. **Map Your Stakeholders**: Identify which personas exist in your organization
2. **Assess Support Levels**: Determine current support level for each stakeholder
3. **Identify Gaps**: Where is engagement lacking?

5. **Monitor**: Track engagement effectiveness monthly

### Adjust for Organizational Culture:
- **Hierarchical Organizations**: Increase executive engagement frequency
- **Flat Organizations**: Increase peer collaboration and transparency
- **Change-Resistant Cultures**: Invest more in change management and user engagement
- **Innovation-Driven Cultures**: Showcase technical innovation and competitive advantage
