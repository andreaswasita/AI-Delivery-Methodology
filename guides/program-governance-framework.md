# Program Governance Framework

## Overview
This document outlines the governance structure, escalation paths, and communication framework for the AI Delivery Program (Athena). The framework ensures effective oversight, decision-making, and stakeholder alignment across all program activities.

---

## 1. Governance Structure

### 1.1 Partnership Governance Layer

#### ELT Steering Committee (Monthly)
- **Purpose**: Executive-level oversight and strategic direction
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Participants**:
  - Executive Leadership Team (ELT) members
  - Program Sponsors
  - Key Business Unit Leaders
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Strategic alignment and investment decisions
  - Remove organizational barriers
  - Approve major program changes
  - Review program ROI and business outcomes
  - Set organizational priorities

#### Business Review Whole Account (Monthly)
- **Purpose**: Comprehensive account-level review and coordination
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Participants**:
  - Account Leadership
  - Program Directors
  - Business Stakeholders
  - Delivery Leads
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Review program health and performance metrics
  - Account-wide resource allocation
  - Cross-program dependencies management
  - Risk and issue escalation from program level
  - Budget and financial oversight

---

### 1.2 Program Management Layer

#### Program Athena
Central coordination hub for all AI delivery initiatives.

##### Industry Solutions Delivery / Athena Delivery
- **Purpose**: End-to-end delivery of industry-specific AI solutions
- **Key Activities**:
  - Project execution and delivery management
  - Sprint planning and tracking
  - Quality assurance and release management
  - Customer acceptance and deployment
- **Leadership**: Delivery Manager / Project Manager
- **Reporting**: Weekly to Program Athena, Monthly to Business Review

##### Industry Solutions Engineering / AI for Customers
- **Purpose**: Technical excellence and solution engineering
- **Key Activities**:
  - Solution architecture and design
  - AI model development and engineering
  - Technical innovation and R&D
  - Platform and infrastructure setup
  - Customer technical enablement
- **Leadership**: Chief Architect / Engineering Lead
- **Reporting**: Weekly to Program Athena, Monthly to Business Review

##### Capability Engagement / Training / Skilling
- **Purpose**: Build and maintain AI capabilities across the organization
- **Key Activities**:
  - Training program delivery
  - Skill assessment and gap analysis
  - Workshop facilitation (Business Envisioning, Solution Envisioning)
  - Knowledge transfer and documentation
  - Community of practice management
- **Leadership**: Capability Lead / Training Manager
- **Reporting**: Weekly to Program Athena, Monthly to Business Review

#### Unified Support / Whole of Account Management
- **Purpose**: Integrated support across the entire account
- **Key Activities**:
  - Cross-program coordination
  - Stakeholder relationship management
  - Unified communication strategy
  - Issue resolution and escalation management
  - Benefits realization tracking
- **Leadership**: Account Manager / Program Director
- **Reporting**: Weekly internal, Monthly to Business Review and ELT

## 2. Escalation Path

### 2.1 Escalation Levels

#### Level 1: Team / Workstream Level
- **Timeframe**: Immediate to 24 hours
- **Scope**: Day-to-day issues, technical blockers, resource conflicts
- **Owner**: Team Lead / Scrum Master
- **Resolution**: Within team or with direct manager
- **Examples**:
  - Technical implementation challenges
  - Sprint-level resource needs
  - Minor scope clarifications

#### Level 2: Program Management Level
- **Timeframe**: 24-72 hours
- **Scope**: Cross-team dependencies, significant risks, budget variances
- **Owner**: Program Manager / Delivery Lead
- **Escalate To**: Program Athena Leadership
- **Resolution**: Program Steering or leadership decision
- **Examples**:
  - Cross-workstream dependencies blocking progress
  - Risks with medium-high impact
  - Resource reallocation between teams
  - Budget overruns within 10-15% threshold

#### Level 3: Business Review / Account Level
- **Timeframe**: 1 week (or next scheduled Business Review)
- **Scope**: Account-wide impacts, major program changes, cross-account resources
- **Owner**: Program Director / Account Manager
- **Escalate To**: Business Review Forum
- **Resolution**: Account leadership decision, may escalate to ELT
- **Examples**:
  - Major scope changes affecting multiple programs
  - Significant budget increases (>15%)
  - Resource conflicts across accounts
  - High-severity risks requiring organizational intervention
  - Customer escalations at executive level

#### Level 4: ELT Steering Committee Level
- **Timeframe**: Up to 1 month (or emergency session)
- **Scope**: Strategic changes, major investments, organizational barriers
- **Owner**: Executive Sponsor
- **Escalate To**: ELT Steering Committee
- **Resolution**: Executive decision, organizational policy change
- **Examples**:
  - Program cancellation or major pivot
  - Investment decisions >$X threshold
  - Organizational structure changes
  - Strategic partnership decisions
  - Legal or compliance issues

---

### 2.2 Escalation Triggers

#### Risk-Based Escalation
| Risk Level | Impact | Escalation Level | Response Time |
|------------|--------|------------------|---------------|
| Low | Minimal impact on timeline/budget (<5%) | Level 1 | 24 hours |
| Medium | Moderate impact (5-15%), workarounds available | Level 2 | 72 hours |
| High | Major impact (>15%), critical path affected | Level 3 | 1 week |
| Critical | Program success at risk, strategic impact | Level 4 | Immediate |

#### Issue-Based Escalation
- **Technical Issues**: Start at Level 1, escalate if unresolved in 2 days
- **Resource Issues**: Start at Level 2, escalate if cross-account impact
- **Budget Issues**: Level 2 for <15%, Level 3 for >15%
- **Scope Changes**: Level 2 for minor, Level 3 for major, Level 4 for strategic
- **Stakeholder Conflicts**: Start at appropriate level based on seniority

## 3. Communication Framework

### 3.1 Communication Cadence

#### Daily Communications
- **Stand-ups**: Team-level (15 minutes)
- **Slack/Teams Updates**: Asynchronous progress updates
- **Blocker Resolution**: Ad-hoc as needed

#### Weekly Communications
- **Program Sync**: All workstream leads + Program Manager (1 hour)
  - Agenda: Progress, risks, dependencies, upcoming milestones
- **Technical Review**: Engineering team (1 hour)
- **Stakeholder Updates**: Written status report to key stakeholders
- **Sprint Reviews**: Team-level demos and retrospectives

#### Bi-Weekly Communications
- **Capability Review**: Training and skilling progress
- **Account Coordination**: Unified Support sync with account teams

#### Monthly Communications
- **Business Review Meeting**: (2 hours)
  - Full program performance review
  - Financial status and forecasting
  - Milestone achievement and upcoming deliverables
  - Risk and issue register review
  - Resource planning for next period
- **ELT Steering Committee**: (1.5 hours)
  - Strategic alignment review
  - Business value realization
  - Major decisions and approvals
  - Executive-level escalations
- **Monthly Newsletter**: Broader stakeholder communication

#### Quarterly Communications
- **Quarterly Business Review (QBR)**: Comprehensive program assessment
- **Benefits Realization Report**: ROI and value delivery
- **Strategic Planning Session**: Next quarter planning

---

### 3.2 Communication Channels

#### Synchronous Channels
- **Microsoft Teams**: Primary collaboration platform
  - General Program Channel
  - Delivery Workstream Channel
  - Engineering Workstream Channel
  - Capability Workstream Channel
  - Executive Updates Channel (read-only for most)
- **Meetings**: Zoom/Teams for all scheduled sessions
- **Emergency Hotline**: For critical escalations

#### Asynchronous Channels
- **Email**: Formal communications, approvals, external stakeholders
- **SharePoint/Confluence**: Document repository, knowledge base
- **JIRA/Azure DevOps**: Work tracking, sprint management
- **Power BI Dashboard**: Real-time program metrics

## 4. Governance Best Practices

### 4.1 Decision-Making Framework
- **Decision Rights**: Clearly defined at each governance level
- **RACI Matrix**: Maintained for all major deliverables
- **Decision Log**: All decisions documented with rationale
- **Escalation Criteria**: Clear thresholds for escalation

### 4.2 Risk Management
- **Risk Register**: Updated weekly, reviewed monthly
- **Risk Assessment**: Probability x Impact matrix
- **Mitigation Plans**: Defined for all high/critical risks
- **Risk Escalation**: Follow defined escalation path

### 4.3 Change Management
- **Change Request Process**: Formal process for scope/budget changes
- **Impact Assessment**: Required for all change requests
- **Approval Authority**: Based on change magnitude
- **Change Log**: All changes tracked and communicated

### 4.4 Quality Assurance
- **Quality Gates**: Defined at each phase
- **Review Process**: Peer reviews, code reviews, architecture reviews
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Clear definition of done
- **Audit Trail**: All key artifacts version-controlled

## 5. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Governance Effectiveness KPIs
- **Decision Velocity**: Average time from issue identification to resolution
- **Escalation Rate**: Number of escalations per level per month
- **Meeting Effectiveness**: % of meetings with clear outcomes
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction**: Quarterly survey scores
- **Communication Reach**: % of stakeholders receiving timely updates

### Program Performance KPIs
- **Schedule Performance**: On-time milestone delivery rate
- **Budget Performance**: Actual vs. planned spend variance
- **Quality Metrics**: Defect rates, customer satisfaction
- **Resource Utilization**: % billable time, resource availability
- **Value Delivered**: ROI, benefits realized vs. planned

## 6. Governance Calendar

### Monthly Rhythm
- **Week 1**: Sprint planning, team kick-offs
- **Week 2**: Mid-sprint check-ins, risk reviews
- **Week 3**: Sprint reviews, retrospectives, program sync
- **Week 4**: Business Review, ELT Steering Committee, reporting

### Quarterly Rhythm
- **Month 1**: Quarterly planning, OKR setting
- **Month 2**: Mid-quarter review, course corrections
- **Month 3**: QBR, benefits realization review, next quarter planning

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

### Executive Sponsor (ELT Level)
- Strategic alignment and organizational support
- Remove executive-level barriers
- Final authority on major program decisions
- Advocate for program at executive level

### Program Director
- Overall program success and delivery
- Stakeholder management at account level
- Resource allocation across workstreams
- Escalation management to Business Review/ELT

### Program Manager
- Day-to-day program execution
- Cross-workstream coordination
- Risk and issue management
- Reporting and communication

### Workstream Leads
- Workstream delivery and quality
- Team management and resource planning
- Technical/functional decisions within scope
- Escalation of cross-workstream issues

### Account Manager (Unified Support)
- Account relationship management
- Customer satisfaction and engagement
- Commercial management
- Account-wide coordination
