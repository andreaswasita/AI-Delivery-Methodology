# Executive Coaching Guide for AI Initiatives

## Document Information

**Purpose**: Guide Microsoft consultants in coaching C-level executives through AI transformation  
**Audience**: Microsoft Consulting Partners, Senior Consultants, Engagement Managers  
**Duration**: Throughout engagement lifecycle  
**Date**: January 2026

---

## Overview

### Purpose of Executive Coaching

Successful AI initiatives require sustained executive commitment and informed decision-making. This guide helps consultants:

- Build trusted advisor relationships with C-level stakeholders
- Navigate organizational politics and resistance
- Guide executives through complex technical and strategic decisions
- Maintain momentum during challenging phases
- Ensure alignment between vision and execution

### The Executive Coaching Challenge

**Common Executive Pain Points:**
- Overwhelmed by AI hype vs. reality
- Uncertain about investment levels and ROI timelines
- Fear of reputational risk from AI failures or bias
- Skepticism based on past technology disappointments
- Pressure from board/investors to "do something with AI"
- Difficulty understanding what's technically feasible
- Concern about workforce disruption and change resistance

**Our Role as Trusted Advisors:**
We bridge the gap between technical complexity and business strategy, helping executives make confident, informed decisions.

**Characteristics:**
- Excited about AI potential
- Thinks big picture and transformation

1. **Financial Business Case**: "Here's the three-year ROI with conservative, likely, and best-case scenarios"

## Executive Coaching Throughout the AI Journey

### Phase 1: Initial Engagement (Weeks 1-2)

**Executive State of Mind:**
- Curious but uncertain
- Evaluating if AI is right for their organization
- Assessing you as trusted advisor
- Wondering about commitment level required

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Build trust and credibility
2. Understand their strategic context
3. Assess organizational readiness
4. Set realistic expectations
5. Secure commitment for discovery

**Key Activities:**

**1:1 Executive Interviews** (60-90 min each)

**Opening:**
> "Thank you for your time. I'd like to understand your strategic priorities, challenges, and thoughts on AI. This isn't a sales pitch‚ÄîI'm here to help you determine if AI makes sense for your organization and, if so, how to approach it successfully."

**Interview Framework:**

**A. Strategic Context (15 min)**
- What are your top 3 strategic priorities?
- What keeps you up at night about your business?
- Where are you feeling competitive pressure?
- What would success look like 2 years from now?

**B. AI Awareness & Expectations (15 min)**
- What's driving your interest in AI?
- What have you seen competitors or peers do?
- What concerns do you have about AI?
- What would make AI a worthwhile investment for you?

**C. Organizational Readiness (15 min)**
- How would you describe your organization's culture toward change?
- Where have you had success with technology transformation?
- Where have you faced challenges?
- Who are your champions and skeptics?

**D. Decision Process (10 min)**
- What would you need to see to move forward?
- Who else needs to be involved in the decision?
- What's your expected timeline?
- What budget parameters should we work within?

**E. Coaching Moment (10 min)**
> "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing... [summarize]. In my experience, organizations at your stage typically face [common challenges]. Here's how we'd approach this..."

**Closing:**
> "I recommend we start with [specific next step]. This will help us [value]. Would that be valuable to you?"

**Post-Interview Actions:**
- Send personalized follow-up email within 24 hours
- Share 1-2 relevant case studies based on their context
- Propose concrete next steps
- Document insights in stakeholder analysis

### Phase 2: Business Envisioning (Weeks 3-4)

**Executive State of Mind:**
- Committed to exploration
- Excited about possibilities
- May have unrealistic expectations
- Concerned about investment
- Wanting team alignment

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Facilitate alignment across leadership team
2. Ground vision in reality
3. Prioritize use cases rigorously
4. Build consensus on approach
5. Secure budget and resources

**Pre-Workshop Executive Coaching Call** (30 min)

**Purpose**: Set up executive sponsor for successful workshop

**Script:**
> "The workshop will be successful if we achieve three things: alignment on vision, prioritization of use cases with clear ROI, and commitment to next steps. Your role is critical. Let me share how you can maximize impact..."

**Coach Them On:**

1. **Setting the tone**
   - Open with strategic context and "why now"
   - Express commitment and urgency
   - Give permission to think big
   - Emphasize this is collaborative, not top-down

2. **Managing dynamics**
   - Watch for dominant voices drowning out others
   - Validate concerns while maintaining momentum
   - Bridge business and technical perspectives
   - Address conflicts constructively

3. **Making decisions**
   - Be prepared to make tough prioritization calls
   - Don't let analysis paralysis set in
   - Commit to resources and timeline
   - Give clear direction on next steps

**During Workshop:**

**Your Coaching Role:**
- Watch executive body language for engagement
- Pull them in when energy drops
- Ask them to share perspectives at key moments
- Redirect to them when decisions needed
- Protect them from getting lost in weeds

**Executive Check-ins** (during breaks):
> "How are you feeling about what's emerging? Is this aligned with what you expected? Any concerns I should address?"

**Post-Workshop Executive Debrief** (30 min - within 48 hours)

**Agenda:**
1. **Reaction Check** (5 min)
   - "What's your take on how that went?"
   - "Are you excited about what emerged?"
   - "Any concerns or surprises?"

2. **Alignment Validation** (10 min)
   - Review prioritized use cases
   - Confirm ROI expectations are realistic
   - Validate timeline and resource commitment
   - Check for any misalignment with exec team

3. **Next Steps Agreement** (10 min)
   - Review detailed plan
   - Confirm budget approval process
   - Set expectations for your communication cadence
   - Discuss how to communicate to broader organization

4. **Coaching Moment** (5 min)
   - Share observations on team dynamics
   - Highlight strengths to leverage
   - Flag risks to manage
   - Provide advice on stakeholder management

### Phase 3: Mobilization & Approval (Weeks 5-6)

**Executive State of Mind:**
- Ready to commit but need final validation
- Facing tough budget decisions
- Concerned about execution risk
- Needing to sell internally (board, peers)
- Impatient to get started

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Secure formal approval and funding
2. Help them sell internally
3. Set realistic expectations
4. Establish governance
5. Prepare them for challenges ahead

**Executive Presentation Coaching**

If they need to present to board or senior leadership, coach them:

**Content Coaching:**
- **Lead with the problem, not the solution**
  - "We're losing $5M annually to X problem" (compelling)
  - Not "We should use AI for X" (technology-first)

- **Use the rule of 3**
  - 3 key problems
  - 3 prioritized solutions
  - 3 main risks with mitigations
  - 3 key metrics

- **Tell a story**
  - Current state (the pain)
  - Desired future state (the vision)
  - The bridge (AI solutions)
  - What we need (the ask)

- **Address concerns proactively**
  - "You might be wondering about [common concern]..."
  - Show you've thought through risks
  - Demonstrate responsible approach

**Delivery Coaching:**
- Practice Q&A for tough questions
- Prepare backup slides for deep dives
- Rehearse opening and closing
- Time the presentation (respect their time)

**Tough Questions to Prepare For:**

**Q: "Why AI? Why not traditional approaches?"**
A: "Traditional approaches can't handle [specific complexity]. AI uniquely enables [specific capability]. We validated this in [proof points]."

**Q: "What if it doesn't work?"**
A: "We're mitigating risk through [phased approach]. Phase 1 requires only $X and will prove [specific value]. We have go/no-go gates before scaling."

**Q: "How do we know this isn't just hype?"**
A: "We've grounded this in [specific ROI projections] based on [data/benchmarks]. We're targeting [conservative metrics]. Here's what [competitor/peer] achieved..."

**Q: "What about job losses?"**
A: "We're focused on augmentation, not replacement. Roles will evolve to [higher value work]. We have a comprehensive change management plan including [reskilling]."

**Q: "Why should we trust Microsoft/you to deliver this?"**
A: "We're bringing [specific expertise/track record]. We've delivered [similar outcomes] for [references]. We're using proven methodology with [specific governance]."

### Phase 4: Execution (Months 2-6)

**Executive State of Mind:**
- Eager for progress and results
- May have unrealistic timeline expectations
- Getting pressure from stakeholders
- Worried when hitting obstacles
- Need reassurance investment is sound

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Manage expectations on timeline and challenges
2. Maintain engagement without micromanaging
3. Navigate obstacles and setbacks
4. Celebrate milestones
5. Keep vision alive

**Regular Executive Check-ins** (Bi-weekly, 30 min)

**Format:**

**1. Quick Wins & Progress (10 min)**
- Share 2-3 concrete accomplishments
- Show visual progress (dashboards, demos)
- Celebrate team contributions
- Build momentum and confidence

**2. Challenges & Decisions Needed (10 min)**
- Surface 1-2 key challenges transparently
- Frame as "need your guidance on..."
- Present options with recommendation
- Make it easy for them to decide

**3. Upcoming Milestones (5 min)**
- Preview what's coming next 2-4 weeks
- Set expectations for what they'll see
- Flag any support needed from them

**4. Coaching Moment (5 min)**
- Check their confidence level
- Address any concerns they're hearing
- Coach on stakeholder management
- Offer strategic advice

**Handling Setbacks**

**When Things Go Wrong** (and they will):

**Bad Example:**
> "We ran into some issues with data quality and the timeline is slipping by 4 weeks."

**Good Example:**
> "I want to give you an early heads up on something. We discovered the customer data is more inconsistent than we initially assessed. This is actually good we found it now rather than in production. We have three options: [A, B, C]. I recommend [B] because [rationale]. This will shift our timeline by 3 weeks, but ensures we deliver quality. I wanted to make sure you're comfortable with this approach before we proceed."

**Key Principles:**
- **Early warning**: Never surprise them
- **Root cause**: Explain what happened clearly
- **Options**: Present alternatives, not just problems
- **Recommendation**: Have a point of view
- **Impact**: Be clear on timeline/budget implications
- **Learning**: Show what we're doing to prevent recurrence

**Coaching Through Resistance**

**When Stakeholders Resist:**

**Diagnose the resistance:**
- Fear of change / job security
- Lack of understanding
- Past negative experiences
- Legitimate concerns about approach
- Political dynamics

**Coach the executive:**
> "I'm noticing resistance from [person/group] around [topic]. This is common and manageable. Based on my assessment, the root cause is [diagnosis]. I recommend you [specific action]. Would you like me to help facilitate?"

**Strategies:**
- **For fear**: Create safe pilots, involve skeptics early
- **For confusion**: Education sessions, show don't tell
- **For past failures**: Acknowledge, show what's different
- **For legitimate concerns**: Listen, adjust approach
- **For politics**: Help them navigate relationships

### Phase 5: Pilot & Validation (Months 6-9)

**Executive State of Mind:**
- Anxious to see results
- Impatient with pilot limitations
- Wanting to scale immediately
- Sensitive to user feedback
- Facing skeptics demanding proof

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Manage impatience to scale prematurely
2. Interpret pilot results objectively
3. Make data-driven go/no-go decisions
4. Plan scaling thoughtfully
5. Build organizational confidence

**Pilot Results Review Session** (90 min)

**Agenda:**

**1. Results Presentation (30 min)**
- Success metrics: actual vs. target
- User feedback and adoption rates
- Technical performance
- Cost analysis
- Lessons learned

**2. Coaching Discussion (30 min)**

**If results are positive:**
> "These results are encouraging. Before we scale, let's ensure we're addressing [key learnings]. I recommend [adjustments] before broader rollout. Let's discuss the scaling plan..."

**Key Questions:**
- What surprised us (positive and negative)?
- What would we do differently?
- Are we ready to scale or need iteration?
- What investments are required for scaling?
- What risks emerged that we need to mitigate?

**If results are mixed:**
> "The results show promise in [areas] but challenges in [areas]. This is actually valuable learning. Let's determine if this is a 'pivot,' 'persevere,' or 'pause' situation..."

**Coach them through:**
- Separating technical issues from fundamental approach
- Distinguishing pilot limitations from real problems
- Making objective vs. emotional decisions
- Considering sunk cost appropriately

**If results are disappointing:**
> "I know this isn't what we hoped for. Let's take a step back and analyze objectively what happened and what our options are..."

**Coach them through:**
- Managing their disappointment productively
- Conducting blameless post-mortem
- Extracting learnings
- Deciding whether to pivot or stop
- Communicating to stakeholders

**3. Go-Forward Decision (30 min)**
- Make clear recommendation
- Outline scaling plan or pivot approach
- Secure decision and resources
- Plan communication strategy

### Phase 6: Scaling & Production (Months 9-12)

**Executive State of Mind:**
- Excited to scale and see full impact
- Impatient with rollout timeline
- Focused on ROI realization
- Concerned about change management
- Wanting to showcase success

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Manage scaling pace (not too fast, not too slow)
2. Drive adoption and change management
3. Track ROI realization
4. Prepare success communication
5. Plan next wave of AI initiatives

**Scaling Strategy Session** (60 min)

**Coach them on key decisions:**

**1. Rollout Approach**
- **Big bang** (all at once) vs. **Phased** (staged rollout)
- When big bang makes sense: Simple change, strong support, urgent need
- When phased makes sense: Complex change, mixed readiness, risk mitigation

**2. Change Management Intensity**
- Light touch: Augmenting existing tools, minimal workflow change
- Moderate: New tools, some workflow changes, training needed
- Heavy: Significant role changes, deep training, champions program

**3. Support Model**
- Help desk and escalation procedures
- Super-user/champion network
- Ongoing training program
- Feedback mechanisms

**ROI Tracking & Communication**

**Monthly Executive Dashboard Review** (30 min)

**Show:**
- Adoption metrics (users, usage, satisfaction)
- Business outcome metrics (cost savings, revenue impact, efficiency)
- Cumulative ROI vs. projection
- Leading indicators of success
- Issues requiring attention

**Coach them:**
> "Here's what the data is telling us... [interpretation]. This is [ahead of/on track with/behind] our projections. Here's what I recommend..."

**Prepare for course corrections:**
- Adoption slower than expected ‚Üí intensify change management
- Usage high but business impact low ‚Üí review success metrics, may need time
- Specific user groups struggling ‚Üí targeted support
- Technical issues ‚Üí rapid response with communication

**Success Communication Coaching**

**When results are strong, help them communicate effectively:**

**Internal Communication:**
- Celebrate the team, not just the technology
- Share user testimonials and stories
- Quantify the impact clearly
- Give credit to stakeholders who supported
- Tease next wave of initiatives

**External Communication** (if appropriate):
- Customer success stories (with permission)
- Industry speaking opportunities
- Press releases with concrete metrics
- Thought leadership articles

**Coach them on messaging:**
- Be specific and authentic
- Share learnings, not just wins
- Acknowledge challenges overcome
- Focus on business value, not technical specs
- Position as journey, not destination

- Satisfied with initial success

**Coaching Objectives:**
1. Maintain momentum and investment
2. Identify next wave of opportunities
3. Build organizational AI capability
4. Scale best practices across organization

**Agenda:**

**1. Value Realization Review (30 min)**
- Cumulative business value delivered
- ROI achieved vs. projected
- Success stories and testimonials
- Comparison to initial business case

**2. Operational Health Check (20 min)**
- System performance and reliability
- User adoption and satisfaction
- Support ticket trends
- Model performance and drift

**3. Strategic Opportunities (30 min)**
- Adjacent use cases identified
- New business problems to address
- Scaling to other business units
- Platform capabilities to build

**4. AI Maturity Advancement (10 min)**
- Current maturity level assessment
- Capabilities developed
- Gaps remaining

**From Project to Platform:**

**Project Thinking** (initial):
- "We built an AI solution for X problem"
- Custom, point solutions
- Limited reusability
- Project-based funding

**Platform Thinking** (maturity):
- "We have an AI capability that solves multiple problems"
- Reusable components and frameworks
- Shared data and ML infrastructure
- Program-based investment

**Coach:**
> "You've proven AI value with [initial use cases]. Now is the time to think bigger‚Äîhow do we make AI a core capability? That means investing in [platform components] that enable multiple use cases. Let's discuss what that looks like..."

**Identifying Next Wave Opportunities:**

**Coaching Questions:**
- Where else in the organization do we see similar problems?
- What adjacent use cases could leverage what we've built?
- Which business units are ready to adopt AI next?
- What infrastructure investments would unlock multiple use cases?
- How do we move from reactive to proactive AI adoption?

## Executive Objection Handling

### Common Objections & Coaching Responses

**Objection 1: "AI is too expensive / We don't have the budget"**

**Poor Response:**
"AI doesn't have to be expensive. We can start small."

**Strong Response:**
> "I understand budget constraints are real. Let's look at this differently‚Äîwhat's the cost of NOT solving [business problem]? You mentioned you're losing $5M annually to [problem]. Even a conservative 30% improvement would be $1.5M in value. Our initial investment is $500K with an 8-month payback. Would you agree that's worth exploring?"

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Reframe from cost to investment
- Quantify cost of inaction
- Show ROI timeline clearly
- Offer phased approach to reduce risk
- Compare to alternative solutions' costs

---

**Objection 2: "We tried AI/ML before and it didn't work"**

**Poor Response:**
"This time will be different."

**Strong Response:**
> "That's a really important point. Can you tell me what happened? [Listen actively]. I hear that [summarize issue]. Here's what we learned from similar situations and how this approach is different: [specific differences]. We're also building in safeguards like [specific mitigations]. Would you be open to a small pilot that addresses those past concerns?"

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Acknowledge the failure without dwelling
- Extract specific lessons learned
- Show what's changed (technology, approach, readiness)
- Demonstrate learning from past
- Offer risk-mitigated pilot

---

**Objection 3: "Our data isn't good enough / ready"**

**Poor Response:**
"We can clean the data."

**Strong Response:**
> "Data quality is a legitimate concern. In fact, one of the first things we do is a comprehensive data assessment. We've found that 'good enough' data often exists‚Äîit's more about knowing what you have and using the right techniques. Let's invest 2 weeks in a data assessment. This will tell us exactly what's feasible and what data improvements would unlock the most value. That's a small investment to derisk a larger initiative. Interested?"

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Validate the concern
- Explain most organizations feel this way
- Propose data assessment as first step
- Show data improvement can be part of project
- Frame as opportunity to fix long-standing issues

---

**Objection 4: "We don't have the right talent / skills"**

**Poor Response:**
"We'll hire data scientists."

**Strong Response:**
> "Talent is a valid consideration. The good news is you don't need to build everything in-house. We typically see a hybrid model work best: [1] Partner with specialists like us for initial delivery and capability building, [2] Develop internal product owners who understand the business, [3] Upskill existing technical staff, [4] Selectively hire for critical roles. This approach gets you moving immediately while building internal capability. Would that address your concern?"

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Offer multiple talent strategies
- Show you can bridge the gap
- Emphasize business knowledge is most critical
- Discuss training and capability building
- Frame as journey, not day-one requirement

---

**Objection 5: "Our organization isn't ready for this level of change"**

**Poor Response:**
"Change management is part of the project."

**Strong Response:**
> "Organizational readiness is actually the #1 predictor of AI success‚Äîso you're asking exactly the right question. Let's assess readiness honestly across culture, skills, and processes. If we find gaps, we address them BEFORE scaling. We might start with a business unit that's more ready, create success, then expand. AI transformation is as much about people as technology. I'd rather go slower and succeed than rush and fail. What aspects of readiness concern you most?"

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Validate that readiness matters critically
- Offer readiness assessment
- Show flexibility in approach
- Emphasize you'll adapt to their pace
- Make them a partner in the solution

---

**Objection 6: "The technology is moving too fast‚Äîmaybe we should wait"**

**Poor Response:**
"You can't afford to wait."

**Strong Response:**
> "I understand that feeling‚ÄîAI is advancing rapidly. Here's how I think about it: waiting for 'perfect' timing means falling behind competitors who are learning now. The organizations that will win aren't those with the newest technology, but those with the most experience applying AI to their business problems. We can build on proven, stable capabilities today while staying positioned to adopt emerging innovations. Plus, the organizational learning‚Äîculture, skills, processes‚Äîtakes time regardless of technology. Starting now gives you that head start. What if we began with stable, proven approaches while monitoring emerging capabilities?"

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Reframe from technology to learning
- Show cost of waiting (competitive disadvantage)
- Emphasize stable vs. bleeding edge options
- Offer evolutionary approach
- Focus on business capability, not just tech

---

**Objection 7: "What about bias, ethics, and regulatory risk?"**

**Poor Response:**

**Coaching the Executive:**
- Take the concern seriously
- Show comprehensive approach
- Reference frameworks and standards
- Offer risk assessment
- Position as competitive advantage
- Show governance structures

## Executive Communication Best Practices

### The 80/20 Rule for Executives

**80% of your communication should be:**
- Business outcomes and value
- Strategic implications
- Risks and mitigations
- Decisions they need to make
- Competitive positioning

**20% of your communication should be:**
- Technical details
- Implementation approach
- Team dynamics
- Process and methodology
- Tools and technology

### Communication Formats by Executive Level

**C-Suite (CEO, CFO, COO):**
- **Format**: Executive summary + optional deep dive
- **Length**: 1-2 pages or 10-15 slides max
- **Frequency**: Monthly or at key milestones
- **Style**: Strategic, high-level, decision-focused
- **Include**: ROI, risks, key decisions, competitive context

**VP/Director Level:**
- **Format**: Detailed reports with executive summary
- **Length**: 5-10 pages or 20-30 slides
- **Frequency**: Bi-weekly or weekly
- **Style**: Balance of strategic and tactical
- **Include**: Progress details, issues, team dynamics, plans

**Green Status** ‚Üí Brief email update

## Building Executive Trust: The Trust Equation

**Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) √∑ Self-Orientation**

### Credibility
**How to build:**
- Demonstrate deep AI and business expertise
- Reference relevant experience and results
- Admit when you don't know something
- Provide insights they can't get elsewhere
- Stay current on industry trends

**Avoid:**
- Overselling your capabilities
- Pretending to have all answers
- Using jargon to sound smart
- Name-dropping without context

### Reliability
**How to build:**
- Do what you say you'll do
- Communicate proactively
- Meet deadlines consistently
- Be responsive to their needs
- Follow through on commitments

**Avoid:**
- Over-promising and under-delivering
- Being late or unprepared
- Waiting for them to ask for updates
- Making excuses

### Intimacy
**How to build:**
- Listen more than you talk
- Remember personal details they share
- Show empathy for their challenges
- Create safe space for concerns
- Be authentic and vulnerable when appropriate

**Avoid:**
- Being transactional
- Only talking about work
- Dismissing their emotions
- Being overly formal

### Low Self-Orientation
**How to build:**
- Focus on their success, not your billing
- Recommend what's right, not what's profitable
- Sometimes advice is "don't do this project"
- Put their interests first
- Give credit to others

**Avoid:**
- Pushing unnecessary scope
- Protecting your project at their expense
- Taking credit inappropriately
- Making it about you

## Advanced Coaching Techniques

### The Coaching Question Framework

**Instead of telling, ask:**

**‚ùå "You should do X"**  
**‚úÖ "What if you tried X? What do you see as pros and cons?"**

**‚ùå "That won't work"**  
**‚úÖ "What would it take for that to work? What obstacles do you foresee?"**

**‚ùå "Here's the answer"**  
**‚úÖ "What options do you see? What's your intuition telling you?"**

**Benefits:**
- Develops their thinking
- Builds ownership
- Surfaces concerns early
- Creates learning, not dependency

### Active Listening Techniques

**Level 1 - Hearing**: You hear words  
**Level 2 - Understanding**: You understand meaning  
**Level 3 - Empathizing**: You feel their emotions  
**Level 4 - Intuiting**: You sense what's unsaid  

**Practice:**
- Pause before responding (count to 3)
- Reflect back what you heard
- Ask clarifying questions
- Notice body language and tone
- Listen for what's NOT being said

**Example:**
> Executive: "I'm not sure the team is ready for this."

**Poor response**: "They'll be fine with training."

**Strong response**: "Tell me more about what makes you uncertain. Is it skills, capacity, or something else?"

### Reframing Techniques

**Turn constraints into opportunities:**

**Constraint ‚Üí Opportunity**
- "Limited budget" ‚Üí "Forces us to prioritize highest ROI"
- "Small team" ‚Üí "Allows us to be agile and iterate quickly"
- "Skeptical stakeholders" ‚Üí "High bar ensures we deliver real value"
- "Legacy systems" ‚Üí "Opportunity to modernize and integrate"

**Practice the "Yes, and..." technique:**

**Executive**: "I'm concerned about timeline"  
**You**: "Yes, timelines are tight, AND we can de-risk by starting with the highest-value use case that proves the approach..."

### Managing Up: Coaching Your Executive to Succeed

**Help them prepare for THEIR stakeholders:**

**Before board meetings:**
- Anticipate tough questions
- Prepare concise answers
- Create backup slides for details
- Rehearse together
- Give them confidence

**Before peer meetings:**
- Brief them on politics
- Arm them with talking points
- Anticipate objections
- Provide competitive intel
- Make them the expert
