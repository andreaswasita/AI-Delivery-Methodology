**Best For**: Overview videos, phase introductions, case studies
**Investment**: $3K-8K per video
**Timeline**: 4-6 weeks per video

**Best For**: Tool demonstrations, phase walkthroughs
**Investment**: $15K-30K equipment + internal time
**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks per video

**Required Equipment**:
- **Camera**: Sony A7 III or similar ($1,500-2,000)
- **Microphone**: Shure SM7B + audio interface ($500)
- **Lighting**: 3-point lighting kit ($400-800)

- **Teleprompter**: Parrot Teleprompter ($200)

**Recommended Setup**:
```
Home Studio Setup:
- Small room with good acoustics (add acoustic panels)
- Professional lighting (key, fill, backlight)
- Green screen for background flexibility
- Desk setup for screen recording
```

**Team Roles**:
- **Content Expert**: Develops script and reviews accuracy
- **Producer**: Manages timeline and coordinates resources
- **Videographer**: Films and directs
- **Editor**: Post-production and graphics
- **Reviewer**: Quality assurance and feedback

**Best For**: Quick updates, FAQ videos, informal walkthroughs

**Minimal Equipment**:
- **Webcam**: Logitech Brio 4K ($200)

- **Screen Recording**: OBS Studio (Free) or Loom ($15/month)

**DIY Video Format**:
- Talking head + screen share
- PowerPoint/Keynote with voiceover

## ğŸ“‹ Video Script Template

### Standard Video Structure

```markdown
## [VIDEO TITLE]
Duration: [Target length]
Audience: [Primary audience]

### HOOK (0:00-0:15)
- Attention-grabbing opening
- State the problem or opportunity
- Promise what viewers will learn

Example:
"Did you know that 70% of AI projects fail to deliver value? 
In the next 12 minutes, I'll show you the exact methodology 
that's helped over 100 organizations achieve AI success..."

### INTRODUCTION (0:15-0:45)
- Who you are (credibility)
- What this video covers
- Why it matters to viewer

Example:
"I'm [Name], and I've led AI transformation projects at 
organizations ranging from healthcare to finance. This video 
walks through Phase 1 of our proven AI Delivery Methodology..."

### CONTENT (Main Body)
Break into 3-5 key sections with clear transitions

#### Section 1: [Topic Name] (XX:XX-XX:XX)
- Key point 1
- Supporting details
- Visual/example
- Quick recap

[Pattern repeats for each section]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION (Last 20%)
- How to apply this in your context
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Next steps to take

### CALL TO ACTION (Final 30 seconds)
- Summary of key takeaways
- What to do next
- Where to find resources
- Engagement request (like, subscribe, comment)

Example:
"To recap: [3 key points]. Download the free templates at 
[link], and let me know in the comments what phase you're 
currently working on. See you in the next video!"

### VISUAL NOTES
[Describe graphics, screen recordings, animations needed]

### SCRIPT NOTES
[Pronunciation guides, emphasis points, timing notes]
```

## ğŸ™ï¸ Production Best Practices

### Recording Tips

**Audio Quality** (Most Important!):
- âœ… Record in quiet room with minimal echo
- âœ… Use pop filter to reduce plosives
- âœ… Maintain consistent distance from mic
- âœ… Record room tone for noise reduction
- âœ… Test levels (aim for -12dB to -6dB)
- âŒ Don't rely on camera mic
- âŒ Avoid rooms with lots of hard surfaces

**Video Quality**:
- âœ… Film in 1080p minimum (4K if possible)
- âœ… Use 24fps or 30fps consistently
- âœ… Ensure good lighting (avoid backlighting)
- âœ… Frame subject with headroom
- âœ… Use tripod or stable surface
- âŒ Don't film in front of windows
- âŒ Avoid distracting backgrounds

**Screen Recording**:
- âœ… Close unnecessary applications
- âœ… Clean up desktop/browser bookmarks
- âœ… Use high resolution (1920x1080)
- âœ… Zoom in on important areas
- âœ… Slow down cursor movements
- âœ… Use annotations to guide attention
- âŒ Don't include personal information
- âŒ Avoid small text that's hard to read

### Editing Best Practices

**Pacing**:
- Cut out pauses longer than 2 seconds
- Remove filler words ("um," "uh," "like")
- Vary shot types every 5-10 seconds
- Use B-roll to cover edits and maintain interest

**Transitions**:
- Use simple cuts (primary transition)
- Crossfade for section changes (1 second max)
- Avoid flashy transitions that distract
- Match action when cutting between shots

**Music & Sound**:
- Use background music sparingly (low volume, 10-15%)
- Fade music out during important points
- Add sound effects for animations/transitions
- Ensure voiceover is always clear priority

**Captions/Subtitles**:
- âœ… Always include captions (accessibility + engagement)
- âœ… Use 1-2 lines maximum
- âœ… Time captions to speech rhythm
- âœ… Include sound effects in brackets [applause]
- Use YouTube's auto-caption as starting point, then edit
